watches

There are two watches in the drawer. One is from my great-grandmother, after she passed (see "note from my grandmother"). I didn't spend much time with her growing up, we lived several states away, but I had always heard stories of her humor. I got to see her one last time, for the first time in nearly a decade, about a year ago for my cousin's wedding. I didn't talk to her much, but it was nice to see her smile one more time.
The other watch is from an antique store in my hometown in Michigan. I brought my boyfriend to visit for the first time, and wanted to show him around to some of my favorite places. We both came across two really beautiful watches in a jewelry case, and bickered over who could buy the other each watch. I felt too bad letting him buy me the one I liked, and vice versa. It was a solid three minutes of convincing on both parts in front of the saleswoman, awkwardly standing behind the case waiting for us to make up our minds. Of course we both bought the other a watch that day.
